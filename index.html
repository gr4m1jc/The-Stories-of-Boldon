🎬 Notable Incidents
The Tales of Thunder Road

With stretches of the road being constructed as early as 1600, Thunder road (formally Newcastle Road) connects Boldon to the nearby town of Fulwell.

By the 1930s, the road became paved route, begining a long history of utilisation— not only for mobility, but as a drag strip.

Follwing national speed laws, in 1971, Thunder road became a national speed limit road, and its future of pushing cars to their limits was set.

In 1995, 'Junior' was able to clock 140mph in his 1.1L car, after cutting a red wire and driving with both feet on the accelerator.

The golden age of Thunder road ended in 2002, when its speed limit was decreased to 50mph, and then recently down to 40mph in some stetches.

According to legend, at 2AM, once you clock 100+mph, the ghost of junior appears alongside you.

👥 Notable People
Denise Robertson (1932–2016)

Television agony aunt on This Morning, who regularly appeared on the show from its inception in 1988 until shortly before her death in 2016, lived in East Boldon.

Daniel James Smith (1864–1911)

Miner who could allegedly lift two stones, both weighing approximately upwards of 43 kilograms, one in each hand. He lived in West Boldon all his life until a mining accident in 1911— injuring him badly, leading to his death two weeks later. There is a memorial of Daniel in the back of Boldon Parish Church.

'Cooky' (1976–)

Born only mere metres away from The River Don— Or the burn as many knows it to be, he spent the majority of his early years submerged in its polluted waters. Leader of the West Boldon Wild Bunch (1987–1993), there was a notable instance in which Cooky was able to complete the 'Holy grail of digger jumping— The Leap over the well' in which he jumped over 12 yards, clearing the Don, and planting his soles in the mud on the other side— being the first (and last) in history to do so. Bypassers chanted his name, thus solidifying Cooky & the WBWB gang as a notorious group of digger jumping legends.

Matty Blah (1939–1945)

Was a young boy who had a pedigree in the destruction of Televisions. One significant incident was at a friend's party, in which he couldn't help but destroy the £1500 pound Television, using nothing but his cranium. Moments after the destruction, most of the party fled, out of fear of being accused in involvement.

Andy (2009–)

Professional prospect cricket player— once hitting a ball 2.4km into the air, with such force a sonic boom was heard from the nearby town of Roker. Eventually, becoming too gluttonous, he reportedly consumed 4kg of curry from 'The Eastern Touch' daily, which blew away his Cricketing dreams. Fortunately, following intensive revision, he managed to achieve his academic ambitions.

The 'Boldon Brawler' (1950–1970)

A local boxer who was known for his aggressive fighting style and his ability to take on opponents much larger than himself. He won several regional championships and remains a legend in the boxing community.

The 'Boldon Bard' (1920–1980)

A local poet and storyteller who was known for his vivid descriptions of life in the Boldons and his ability to capture the essence of the community in his work. His poetry remains a cherished part of local heritage.

Junior (1975–unknown)

Broke the world record for fastest speed clocked in a car— 140mph, on Boldons 'Newcastle Road'. His name lives on in Thunder Road legend.
